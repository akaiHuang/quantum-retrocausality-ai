How it works: In the Schwarzschild metric, the coordinate speed of light varies with distance from a massive body. The effective refractive index is n(r) = 1/(1 - r_s/r), meaning light slows down near the mass.

Light rays bend toward the mass following Fermat's principle (just like light bends in a glass lens). The radial speed is c_r = c(1 - r_s/r) and the tangential speed is c_t = c√(1 - r_s/r).

At the photon sphere (1.5 r_s), photons orbit the mass. At the event horizon (r_s), the effective speed drops to zero — light cannot escape.
